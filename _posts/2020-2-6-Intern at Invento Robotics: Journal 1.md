Hello World!

Invento Robotics, sounds familiar? The majority of the people I have interacted have heard of this start-up for the first time. So there are two ways I go about introducing this start-up to them

1. “ Remember when Ivanka Trump came to India for the GES summit, there was this robot named MITRA which went on to welcome Ivanka and Modi”

2. “ You might know Balaji Vishwanathan on Quora, it is his start-up”

This introduction generally works out, but if it does not we have to move on. As an intern, there is so much learning I can take out from here. I have had a lot of people asking me what happens out there, is it fun, is work tough, are weekends holidays and so much more! So I decided to create this series of blog posts and share what all I am allowed to!

First I would love to answer those set of people who call MITRA or MITRI a toy on wheels and have a lot to complain about. On hearing the word ROBOT if your imagination pulls down a picture of Rajnikanth or Boston Dynamics bots jumping all around, then you should STOP right there! I have seen Balaji Sir’s answer to this question and I stand on the same lines. MITRA and MITRI are not those billion-dollar bots who can go about jumping. The purpose of this company is to serve robots that can interact with humans and serve commercial and domestic spaces. Regarding the toy part, no toy moves around without human assistance. No toy remembers the last time you came to the office, greets you and guides you to where you want to go. No toy can have long conversations with you. Mitra and Mitri have different purposes they are not defense bots that come at the stake of billions these are affordable domestic and commercial bots, which can be used as receptionists, waiters, guides and also to steal the show like it did in GES.

Next, I would like to specify on the team, For a lot of people, Invento is just about Balaji Sir, since they know him through Quora. But as an Intern what I realized is MITRA is a product of immense teamwork. The way this team works is just AMAZING! They gel so well together, that sometimes I feel nothing can go wrong for them. The way they have sessions at the end of each day to discuss the individual progress and take in constructive criticism is simply phenomenal. A point to note down for all students who are looking into entrepreneurship is to not just get an intelligent set of people but to get the combination of people who are passionate, willing to work and also contribute to the culture of the workplace. By culture what I mean is not a race , religion-based culture but the way they understand people and adapt accordingly. As an intern some really key points I noted down in my notebook in terms of teamwork especially if you are planning to start some enterprise is

1. The final product is not an individual effort but a collective effort it is atmost important to know what every individual in the office is working on, such that your contribution is in SYNC with certain parameters with the team.

2. Have a regular meetup everyday before leaving the office to see how every individual has progressed and take constructive criticism.

3. Maintain a notebook and pen, if you believe you can remember everything you want to do, SORRY! you cant. You can also have sticky paper notes to remind you of what you have to do.

Next talking about the man himself Balaji Vishwanathansir.

For sure he is a voracious reader, He can literally talk about anything on this planet. CEO is not an easy post to hold, and it requires great experience to be one. Some points I have noted from Balaji Sir through observation and was keen to share on are

1. Stay Patient. Your team is bound to make mistakes that is exactly when you learn to make the product better. But the moment when the leadership team gets angry on the team for committing mistakes, nobody is ready to commit mistakes and there is a lot of learning and innovation killed in the process.

2. Never pull the sad face, Great leaders never pull down the morale of the team. If cricket is your cup of tea, You might know Brendon McCullum, I find very similar instincts in Balaji Sir, irrespective however good or bad things might be progressing always create that positive ambiance. Balaji Sir keeps walking around and talking to interns and full-time members giving advice and checking how things are progressing and have a small laugh.

3. Again like Brendon McCullum, he is very aggressive in getting work done. By aggressive in the sense, he plans things well ahead and knows perfectly when and what to do.

So currently about my stay and how I am getting things done here, I live in a PG hardly a few minutes walk from the office. I have this very nice table and get coffee two times a day. The best part of the office is lunch. Lunch is where most of the networking happens. All the interns, full-time employees, and Balaji Sir have lunch together and especially as an Intern this is where most of the learning happens (leadership and non-technical staff).

All I can tell is this is turning out to be great so far. I have been meeting great people and some really great minds. Oh! I totally forgot, yes Sunday is a holiday! If you get a chance never think twice just come down and work here, you can cherish you are being part of something great.
