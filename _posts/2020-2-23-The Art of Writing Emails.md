Ever since the return from my internship at GeorgiaTech and acceptance as a research Intern at IISc Bangalore, I have had a lot of messages, emails, DM's asking one very important question "Hey, How did you land with an interview call in the first place?". Read along, to know how I had applied and what I learnt in the prcess which might help you.

Recently I recieved a message on Whatsapp from my junior, he wanted help in drafting an email to a professor he was intrested in working with. Here is the email.

*Subject : Possible Undergraduate Research Opportunities

*Dear Dr.LMNOP,*

*I am ABC, studying 3rd year CSE undergraduate at XYZ university. I have a perfect cgpa of 10 and hold a merit scholarship. I have a great intrest in machine learning and completed courses from Andrew NG on coursera. I have noticed your labs work on machine learning based projects and wanted to request if you can provide an opportunity for me to intern in your lab during the summer of 2020. An internship under your guidence would act as a perfect platform for my future research ambitions.*

Lets break down the above email into its pro's and cons. Before we go any further these are purley based on my experiences and this discussion is not based on any ground truth or comprehensive set of rules. These are based on my personal intution and experiences. 

**Subject:** 
* The subject appear to vague. The whole point of a subject is convey the curx of your email in one single line. Here the subject seems more like a question rather then conveying the core point. 

**Address**
* The term 'Dear' is very subjective to the reciptent. There are numerous people who associate the word 'Dear' to a personal context. I prefer dropping the word 'Dear'. 

**Uppercase and Lowercase**
* Using acronyms stick to capitals. 'cgpa' is a big 'NO NO'.

**Content**
  * '3rd Year CSE undergraduate', an email is an oppotunity to show your best work. Whenever you get the opportunity drop of words which assert things that are obvious. Professors are smart people, they for sure will make out you are an undergraduate.
  
  * The more we relate the way we talk the way we write, the messier it gets. When people talk, we talk with a lot of emotion and add all the adjectives we know. 'perfect cgpa of 10' is a very emotional statement. 'CGPA of 10' still has your CGPA 10 and also conveys the exact same point. The word perfect is rebundant and an emotional weight.

**EDIT!**
This is going to be the fun part, let us edit the same email in a manner it sounds a bit better and eventually move on to sample emails. 

*Subject : Potential Undergraduate Research Inter : Summer 2020

*Hello Dr.LMNOP,*

*This is ABC from the School of Computer Science Department at XYZ University. I have had the opportunity to come across your work while searching for motivation on developing a research apptidude for Machine learning. Apart from my coursework I have taken courses from Stanford's lectures delieved by Andrew NG on Machine Learning. A CGPA of 10 and a merit scholarship reflect my desire to learn and apply. For the summer of 2020 I am keen on joining your lab as a research intern to put my learning to test. I beleive concepts such QRST, UVW I picked up in the course will help me contribute to projects and reserach activities in your lab. In specific project DEF has dragged my intrest and I am looking forward to applying QRST on DEF and analyse the results.A research internship under your wing, will act as a perfect platform for me to build my career in research and academia. I look forward to hear back from you* 

So this is what we have from what student ABC had to showcase. Let us talk about the differences we saw in the two emails. 

* 'I have had the opportunity to come across your work', this shows you are genuinely intrested in working with a professor and his lab. You have gone through homepage, papers and understood what the professor is working on. This is important because labs look to hire people who are familiar with their work.
