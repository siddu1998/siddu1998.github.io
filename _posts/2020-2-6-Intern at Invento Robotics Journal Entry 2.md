“Time Flies,” they said and here it went on to fly faster than ever. With so much learning I had a great experience putting my learning and character to test. I will scribble down the last phase of my internship at Invento 

When you work with the same set of exciting people for 8 hours a day over a period of nearly two months, they turn into family. Family always helps you understand different phases of life and how to come across them. I underwent a very similar period here, where I had senior developers and managers coming up and asking my future plans and helping me out how I should tackle them. I learned how as computer science students I have to hunt for problems and come up with solutions. I learned we need to make solutions that complement business models and not contradict them. I can go on, but the crux is me able to understand how meeting the market demands is a totally different ball game which I on a personal note never came across in my university till date.

On the technical front the final stage of the internship involved in actually moving everything to production, Be it Computer Vision-based customer engagement, Converting 8 channel recording into a single-channel recording, ROS based Navigation and the Kinect based obstacle detection, everything had to be pushed for integration with the bot and honestly, I felt this was the toughest part during the whole internship since it sounds easy to push something working on your computer into production but new problems keep rising very often which can be quite demotivating and requires you to solve the issue dealing with one variable at a time. I have given my best during this phase, and believe I could have done a bit better on the ROS front had I spent more time with the hardware section. Learning and gaining comfort with ROS opened up a totally new direction and helps me use ROS in my future projects or papers.

What fun is it to work all the time and have no fun! Invento knows this better than any start-up and to take a break regular “fun”-days are always on the cards. All of them gather up cook food, watch a movie and then play an unlimited amount of counter strike. I will post some pictures, where we all gathered up at CTO’s home, get ready for some crazy surprises.

HouseFull Invento Robotics Summer-2018!
My core intentions for writing this series of internship stories were

To give an insight into how exciting it is to work in start-ups.
With a lot of start-ups in the Delhi belt rising which involve a lot of hardware, it is very difficult to meet cost demands. This series of blogs I was keen on throwing light on how successful start-ups which involve a combination of hardware and software function.
As a college student I never understood why do clubs in my college even have a sales and marketing team apart from putting up posters, but today I understand they are the ones who are firmly in the driving seat. Invento has an Avengers set of people in its sales and marketing team which is one of the primary reasons it has its own share of success.
I was also interested in sharing leadership traits I learned and observed from senior developers, managers, vice-presidents and the CEO himself.
Most of all I was keen on getting peers, friends, juniors and whoever interested to contribute to developing products that solve problems and create an IMPACT.
