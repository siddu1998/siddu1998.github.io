A New Generation of Currency

Concept: Currency as Storytelling

Currency notes are more than monetary instruments—they are powerful storytelling devices that circulate through millions of hands daily, carrying narratives of identity, heritage, and aspiration. Each note becomes a canvas for communicating values, honoring achievements, and inspiring future generations. This design reimagines currency as a medium for cultural dialogue and contemporary role modeling.

Unlike traditional mediums that require active engagement, currency enters the lives of people naturally—in pockets, wallets, and transactions. This ubiquity creates unprecedented opportunities for representation. When individuals from diverse backgrounds, professions, and contributions are featured on currency, they become part of the daily visual landscape, normalizing excellence and achievement across all domains of human endeavor.

New Role Models, New Stories

By featuring figures like Lata Mangeshkar (music), A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (science), and Subhas Chandra Bose (leadership), this currency celebrates excellence beyond political or traditional domains. These selections inspire a generation to see themselves reflected in diverse fields—from arts and sciences to innovation and social change. Every transaction becomes a subtle reminder that greatness takes many forms, and that the next generation can find their role models not just in history books, but in their hands during everyday moments. When young people hold currency featuring scientists, artists, and leaders who look like them or share their aspirations, it plants seeds of possibility—transforming each note into a story of "this could be you."

The vertical orientation further amplifies this storytelling potential. With more visual real estate dedicated to portraits and symbolic elements, each note becomes a small gallery—a moment of recognition and connection. The format invites viewers to pause, examine details, and connect with the stories embedded in the design. In a world where attention is fragmented, currency design that embraces storytelling creates meaningful touchpoints, reminding us of who we honor, what we value, and the stories we choose to tell about ourselves.

Design Decisions

Every element of these currency notes has been meticulously chosen to balance aesthetic beauty, functional clarity, and inclusive accessibility. The design system employs a carefully curated palette, modern typography, and innovative accessibility features.

Color Palette

Each denomination features a distinct, vibrant color that serves both aesthetic and functional purposes—creating instant recognition while celebrating India's rich visual heritage.

Typography System

A dual-font system ensures optimal legibility and modern aesthetic while supporting clear hierarchy and functional requirements.

Numerals — Inter: Inter provides exceptional clarity for numerals at all sizes. Its geometric precision ensures instant recognition of denominations, even from distances or at angles. Used for primary denominations: ₹ 100

Text — Roboto Mono: Roboto Mono's monospaced character provides uniform spacing that enhances readability for secondary information. Its technical precision complements Inter's geometric clarity. Used for secondary text: One Hundred Rupees

Accessibility: Braille Integration

Inclusivity is embedded at every level of the design. Tactile Braille elements enable visually impaired users to identify denominations through touch, making currency truly accessible to all.

Universal Design Principle: The vertical format provides optimal space for Braille placement without disrupting the visual design. Braille dots are positioned strategically along the note's edges, ensuring easy access while maintaining aesthetic integrity. This integration transforms currency from a visual-only medium into a multi-sensory experience, honoring the principle that good design serves everyone.

Iconic Portraits

Each denomination features a prominent, vertically-oriented portrait of an iconic Indian figure, rendered in monochrome tones matching the note's color palette. The vertical format allows these portraits to dominate the design, honoring these figures as national icons.

  • ₹100 - Subhas Chandra Bose: Military uniform portrait emphasizing leadership and determination. Positioned in the lower two-thirds of the note, his distinctive features—glasses, cap, and determined gaze—create instant recognition.
  • ₹50 - Lata Mangeshkar: Close-up portrait celebrating India's musical heritage. Featuring traditional bindi and classical pose, this portrait honors one of India's most beloved cultural figures.
  • ₹500 - A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: Warm, approachable portrait with distinctive hairstyle embodying scientific achievement and national pride. His characteristic features make him immediately recognizable.
  • Forehead Emphasis: Each portrait's distinctive forehead features (tilaka/bindi, hair styling, glasses) create instant recognition and serve as natural security elements.
  • Cultural Significance: The vertical format enables larger, more detailed renderings that honor these subjects appropriately, making their contributions visible and celebrated.
Design Rationale

The iconic foreheads and facial features serve as both aesthetic elements and security features—difficult to replicate accurately, they provide both beauty and protection against counterfeiting.

Language & Communication

The design prioritizes clarity through a thoughtful approach that ensures accessibility while maintaining visual elegance. Every element is designed to communicate effectively across cultural boundaries.

  • English Primary: All denominations shown in English numerals and text ensures international recognition and supports global usability.
  • Universal Symbols: The ₹ symbol is universally understood across languages, transcending written communication barriers.
  • Visual Hierarchy: Numbers and currency symbols are language-independent, supporting accessibility for diverse users.
  • Cultural Context: Traditional patterns in the top border incorporate visual storytelling that transcends language barriers, communicating heritage through design.

Security & Holographic Features

Multiple layers of security features are integrated seamlessly into the aesthetic design. Each element serves dual purposes—beauty and protection—creating a currency that is both visually stunning and technologically secure.

  • Intaglio Printing: Raised textures on portraits and key elements are detectable by touch, providing tactile authentication without compromising aesthetics.
  • Optical Variable Ink: The top decorative band features color-shifting inks that change when tilted, creating a dynamic visual experience while serving as a security feature.
  • Watermarks: Subtle denomination numbers visible when held against light provide an elegant authentication method that doesn't interfere with the design.
  • Micro-printing: Traditional patterns contain microscopic text identifying the currency, integrating security into decorative elements.
  • Security Thread: Embedded metallic thread visible as a continuous line on the front, positioned to enhance rather than disrupt the visual flow.
  • Latent Images: Hidden elements revealed when viewed at specific angles provide layered security that rewards careful examination.
  • UV Features: Fluorescent patterns visible under ultraviolet light for authentication, invisible under normal conditions to maintain design purity.
Traditional Patterns as Security

The colorful top border patterns aren't merely decorative—they serve as complex security features that are extremely difficult to reproduce accurately. The intricate designs combine cultural authenticity with technological sophistication.

Vertical Orientation: Design Benefits

The decision to adopt a vertical orientation—similar to currency designs in Canada, Switzerland, and other modern economies—represents a fundamental shift in currency design philosophy. This orientation offers numerous advantages over traditional horizontal layouts.

  • Portrait Dominance: The vertical format allows iconic portraits to occupy the full height, creating a powerful, memorable presence. The vertical space enables larger, more detailed renderings that honor the subjects appropriately.
  • Modern Aesthetic: Vertical orientation aligns with contemporary design trends seen in digital interfaces, mobile devices, and modern architecture. It signals forward-thinking and innovation.
  • Natural Grip: The vertical format matches how people naturally hold currency notes, making them feel more ergonomic and comfortable in hand. The aspect ratio feels more balanced during handling.
  • Improved Legibility: Text flows naturally top-to-bottom, matching reading patterns. Denominations are immediately visible when notes are stacked or held naturally, without needing to rotate.
  • Better Stacking: Vertical notes stack more efficiently in wallets, cash drawers, and ATMs. They're easier to count and organize, reducing handling time for both individuals and businesses.
  • Enhanced Security: The vertical layout allows security features to be positioned more strategically. Watermarks and security threads can run vertically, making them more prominent and harder to replicate.
  • Cultural Storytelling: The vertical format enables a clear narrative structure—top patterns represent heritage, middle sections show value, and bottom portraits honor icons, creating a visual journey.
  • Digital Adaptability: Vertical orientation translates better to mobile banking apps, digital wallets, and payment interfaces, ensuring consistency across physical and digital representations.

Following the examples of countries like Canada (which introduced vertical notes in 2018) and Switzerland (known for striking vertical designs), this orientation represents a commitment to modern design that balances aesthetic beauty, functional usability, and technological innovation.
