The Pattu Border

In South Indian silk sarees, the pattu border is the ornate woven edge — temples, peacocks, elephants — each motif carrying generations of meaning.

When I first walked into a saree shop in India, the shopkeeper explained every detail: the weight of the silk signals the occasion, the width of the border marks formality, the motifs connect to regional identity. A masterclass in human-centered design.

This stripe is a tribute to that craft — a reminder that the best design communicates richness through thoughtful detail.

About

I grew up between cultures: Telugu at home, English at school, Hindu mythology at bedtime, video games on weekends. That gap is where most of my research lives. I keep finding the same problem: tools and systems that assume a default user who doesn't match the person in front of me. My work is usually an attempt to close that gap, whether it's a climate game that speaks to someone's grandmother or a research platform that doesn't require a bilingual vendor to hear from players in Vietnam.

I have a PhD in HCI from UC Santa Cruz, spent time at Meta building new generative research methods, and most recently did an internship at Xbox designing AI tooling for their global research team. The through-line across all of it is the same question: who is the system actually designed for, and what do they need that it isn't giving them yet?
